## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have:

<Callout kind="info">

- A valid email address for account verification
- Access to a data source like Google Sheets or PostgreSQL
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Safari recommended)

</Callout>

## Create and Verify Your Account

Sign up for Baza Analytics in under two minutes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the signup page" icon="external-link">
    Navigate to [https://dashboard.bazaanalytics.com/signup](https://dashboard.bazaanalytics.com/signup).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your details" icon="user">
    Provide your email, create a strong password, and accept the terms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify your email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for a verification email from Baza Analytics and click the link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log in" icon="log-in">
    Return to the dashboard and sign in with your verified credentials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connect Your First Data Source

Baza Analytics supports multiple sources. Choose your preferred method below.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Google Sheets" icon="file-spreadsheet">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Authorize Google" icon="apple">
        Click `Connect Google Sheets` and grant permissions.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select sheet">
        Choose your spreadsheet and specific sheet (e.g., `Sales Data`).
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Callout kind="tip">
      Ensure your sheet is publicly accessible or shared with your Baza account.
    </Callout>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="PostgreSQL" icon="database">
    In the dashboard, select `Add Database` and enter these details:

    ```
    Host: your-db-host.example.com
    Port: 5432
    Database: baza_analytics
    User: your_username
    Password: `YOUR_DB_PASSWORD`
    ```

    Test the connection before saving.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Run Your First Query

With data connected, explore it immediately.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Query Editor" icon="edit-3">
    Click `New Query` in the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write and execute" icon="play">
    Enter a query and click `Run`. View results in real-time charts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Here is a sample query on sales data:

<CodeGroup tabs="SQL,Natural Language">
  ```sql
  SELECT 
    region, 
    SUM(revenue) as total_revenue,
    AVG(units_sold) as avg_units
  FROM sales
  WHERE date > '2024-01-01'
  GROUP BY region
  ORDER BY total_revenue DESC;
  ```

  ```text
  Show total revenue and average units sold by region for sales after January 1, 2024, sorted by revenue.
  ```
</CodeGroup>
